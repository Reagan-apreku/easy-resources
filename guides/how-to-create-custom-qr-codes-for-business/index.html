How to Create Custom QR Codes for Your Business

Published May 13, 2026 • 5 min read

In today's mobile-first world, bridging the gap between physical and digital experiences is critical for any business. The easiest and most effective way to do this? Quick Response (QR) codes.

Whether you're running a restaurant, marketing a real estate listing, or launching a new product, custom QR codes provide a frictionless way for your customers to interact with your brand instantly.

1. Choose the Right Type of QR Code

Not all QR codes are just website links. Depending on your business need, you can encode various types of data:

  • URLs: The most common type. Direct users to your website, menu, or social media.
  • vCards (Digital Business Cards): Perfect for networking. When scanned, it prompts the user to save your contact details directly to their phone.
  • WiFi Networks: Ideal for cafes and hotels. Guests scan the code and instantly connect to your WiFi without typing a password.

2. Customize with Your Brand Colors

The days of boring black-and-white QR codes are over. A custom QR code that matches your brand colors increases scan rates by up to 30%. However, there is one golden rule: always ensure high contrast. The foreground (the pattern) must always be significantly darker than the background, otherwise smartphone cameras will struggle to read it.

3. Add Your Logo (Error Correction)

Embedding a logo in the center of your QR code instantly builds trust. But how does the code still work if part of it is covered up? This is due to a technology called Error Correction.

QR codes use the Reed-Solomon error correction algorithm. By setting your code to a high error correction level (Level H), up to 30% of the code can be damaged or obscured (by a logo) without affecting its scannability. Professional generators handle this automatically.

4. Download in the Correct Format

Finally, make sure you use the right file format for the job:

  • PNG: Great for digital use (social media posts, websites, emails).
  • SVG (Scalable Vector Graphics): Crucial for print. SVG files can be scaled to the size of a billboard without ever losing quality or becoming pixelated.
